üè∞ Disney Disability Access Service (DAS)

What is DAS?

DAS is designed for guests who have difficulty waiting in conventional queues due to a developmental disability such as autism. It allows guests to wait outside the queue and return at a designated time.

üè∞ Other Disney Accessibility Services

Wheelchair & ECV Rentals

Available at park entrances. Wheelchairs ~$12/day, ECVs ~$50-70/day. Reserve ECVs early - they run out!

Rider Switch

One adult waits with someone who can't ride, then swaps with rest of party without waiting again.

Handheld Captioning & Audio Description

Devices available at Guest Relations for guests who are deaf/hard of hearing or blind/low vision.

Sign Language Interpretation

Available at select shows on certain days. Request 14+ days in advance.

Service Animals

Service dogs welcome in most areas. Relief areas throughout parks.

Sensory Guides

Downloadable guides showing sensory effects (loud noises, darkness, flashing lights) for each attraction.

üé¢ Universal Accessibility Services

Attraction Assistance Pass (AAP)

Similar to Disney's DAS. For guests who cannot wait in traditional queues. Get a return time, come back later. Register at Guest Services.

Wheelchair & ECV Rentals

Available near park entrances. Standard wheelchair ~$12, ECV ~$50-70. Third-party rentals often cheaper.

Child Switch

Same as Disney's Rider Switch - adults take turns waiting with non-riders.

Assistive Listening & Captioning

Devices available at Guest Services for hearing impaired guests.

Rider's Guide for Guests with Disabilities

Detailed PDF available online showing which rides accommodate wheelchairs, transfer requirements, etc.
